Our Curriculum

DemystifyAI Chicago's curriculum is designed to build practical AI literacy skills for everyday community members. Our modules can be delivered in sequence or adapted to meet the specific needs and constraints of partner organizations.

Module 1: AI Foundations

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand what AI actually is (and isn't) in everyday terms
  • Recognize AI in daily life experiences and use cases
  • Distinguish between AI myths and realities

Topics

  • What AI Really Means: Simple definition: AI as pattern recognition and prediction. Common examples: autocorrect, GPS navigation, social media feeds. The basic building blocks of how it works (e.g. trained on data, predictive vs. generative)
  • AI in Your Daily Life: Interactive exercise: Identifying AI in participants' routines. Smartphones, streaming services, online shopping recommendations. Banking fraud detection, email spam filtering
  • Debunking Myths: Importance of human involvement. AI is not magic or all-knowing. AI is not "objective". Discussion: what do participants already know about AI?

Module 2: Protecting Yourself and Engaging with AI

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify common AI-powered scams and fraud attempts
  • Understand deepfakes and manipulated media

Topics

  • Media Literacy, Algorithmic Recommendations, and AI-Enabled Scams and Manipulation: Voice and video deepfakes and media manipulation. AI-generated phishing and scams (e.g. email scams). Algorithmic recommendations and filter bubbles. Discussion: Authenticity verification checklist
  • Privacy and Data Misuse: Manage privacy settings. Data ownership rights and navigating digital consent (cookies, terms of service, etc.). Discussion: Knowing your rights
  • Understanding Applications of Algorithms in the Social and Public Sectors: In employment, healthcare, law enforcement, etc. Algorithmic usage in government settings & how they affect you everyday. Appealing automated decisions. Discussion: ask critical thinking questions about fairness, transparency, and accountability

Module 3: AI as a Tool - Practical Applications for Community Benefit

Learning Outcomes

  • Explore positive uses of AI tools for personal and community needs
  • Hands-on experience with accessible AI applications
  • Understand limitations and responsible use of AI

Topics Covered

  • Prompt Engineering: What is a prompt? Strategies for writing effective prompts. Hallucinations and evaluating LLM responses.
  • Language and Communication Tools: Translation apps for multilingual communities. Text-to-speech and speech-to-text tools. Document analysis (e.g. uploading a pdf and automatic translation)
  • Information and Learning Tools: Use in brainstorming. AI writing assistants. AI tutoring. Accessibility features (image description, closed captioning). Agentic AI and use in daily workflows and searches
  • Hands-On Workshop: Experiment with tools. Discussion of benefits and limitations. How could these tools help your family or community organization? What concerns do you have about using AI tools?
